# SACCO CONSTITUTION

**COMPANY EMPLOYEES SACCO** (Internal Savings and Credit Cooperative)

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the rules, structure, and operational guidelines of the Company Employees SACCO, established to promote a savings culture and provide financial assistance to employees.

## 2. Objectives

The SACCO aims to:

- Encourage regular savings among members
- Provide affordable and accessible loans
- Promote financial discipline and stability
- Improve the economic well-being of members

## 3. Membership

### 3.1 Eligibility

Membership is open to all employees of the company, including:

- Drivers
- Security personnel
- Managers
- Casual workers

### 3.2 Registration Requirements

To become a member, an individual must:

- Complete a membership application form
- Pay a one-time registration fee (KES 500–1000)
- Agree to abide by this constitution

### 3.3 Termination of Membership

A member may exit the SACCO by:

- Providing a 30-day written notice
- Clearing all outstanding loans

Savings will be refunded after loan clearance.

## 4. Contributions

### 4.1 Contribution Structure

Due to varying pay cycles:

- Monthly employees: Contribute once per month
- Casual employees: Contribute every 15 days

### 4.2 Contribution Options

**Option A: Fixed Contribution**
- Monthly employees: KES 2,000/month
- Casual workers: KES 1,000 every 15 days

**Option B: Percentage-Based (Recommended)**
- Members contribute 5–10% of their earnings

### 4.3 Rules

- Contributions are mandatory
- Late contributions attract a penalty (e.g., KES 100)
- Voluntary additional savings are allowed

## 5. Savings Policy

- All contributions are treated as member savings
- Savings cannot be withdrawn unless:
  - The member exits the SACCO
  - Special approval is granted for emergencies

## 6. Loan Policy

### 6.1 Eligibility

A member qualifies for a loan if:

- They have been active for at least 3 months
- They have consistent contributions

### 6.2 Loan Limits

- Maximum loan = 3× member's total savings

### 6.3 Guarantors

- Minimum of 2 guarantors required
- Guarantors must:
  - Be active members
  - Have sufficient savings

### 6.4 Interest Rate

- Interest charged at 1–2% per month
- Reducing balance method is recommended

### 6.5 Loan Types (Optional)

- Emergency loans (small, short-term)
- Development loans (larger, long-term)

## 7. Loan Repayment

### 7.1 Repayment Period

Loans are repayable within 3 to 12 months

### 7.2 Repayment Method

- Monthly employees: Monthly deductions
- Casual workers: Deductions every 15 days

### 7.3 Default Management

In case of default:

- Guarantors are responsible for repayment
- Member savings may be used to offset the loan

## 8. Penalties

- Late contribution: KES 100
- Late loan repayment: Additional interest penalty
- Loan default: Recovery from guarantors or savings

## 9. Management Structure

The SACCO shall be managed by elected officials:

- Chairperson
- Treasurer
- Secretary
- Loan Committee

### 9.1 Roles

- **Chairperson**: Oversees operations
- **Treasurer**: Manages finances
- **Secretary**: Maintains records and communication
- **Loan Committee**: Reviews and approves loans

## 10. Meetings

- Regular meetings shall be held monthly or quarterly
- Key agenda items include:
  - Financial reports
  - Loan approvals
  - Member concerns

## 11. Profits and Dividends

- Profits generated from interest shall be distributed annually
- Dividends are allocated based on member savings

## 12. Record Keeping

The SACCO shall maintain accurate records of:

- Member details
- Contributions
- Loans and repayments

Members shall have access to their financial records.

## 13. Special Provisions

### 13.1 Casual Workers

- Lower loan limits
- Shorter repayment periods
- More frequent contributions

### 13.2 Permanent Employees

- Higher loan eligibility
- Longer repayment periods

## 14. Risk Management

To ensure sustainability:

- Loans must have guarantors
- Loan approvals require committee review
- Exposure limits must be enforced
- An emergency fund may be established

## 15. Amendments

- This constitution may be amended through member consensus
- Changes must be approved during an official meeting

## 16. Adoption

This constitution becomes effective upon approval by the founding members of the SACCO.

---

### Notes

- This SACCO begins as an internal company initiative
- It may later be formalized and registered if it grows
